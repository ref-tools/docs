# Good Night, Moon

Good night, moon,
hanging low and bright,
a lantern left burning
at the edge of the night.

Good night, stars,
scattered like scattered seeds,
silver and patient
above the whispering reeds.

Good night, wide sky,
your darkness soft as wool,
wrapped around the rooftops,
quiet, deep, and full.

Good night, small bird
tucked beneath your wing,
good night to every living thing
that's done with wandering.

Good night, old river,
curling to the sea,
good night to every mystery
that watches over me.

The moon pulls the tides
and lulls the world to rest—
let your eyelids grow heavy,
let your heartbeat slow its quest.

For the night is a harbor,
and sleep is the shore,
and the stars keep their vigil
till the sun finds the door.

Good night, dear dreamer,
let the darkness be kind.
The moon holds its lantern
for the peace you will find.
